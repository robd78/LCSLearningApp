An interactive learning tool built from the 2025 LCSP Standard Protocol and QA Standards. For radiologist self-directed learning.

How to use this tool

This is a self-paced learning aid for radiologists reporting on the NHS Lung Cancer Screening Programme (LCSP). It has five working parts:

Nodules

A guided decision tree. Pick baseline or incident round, then describe the nodule — the tool walks you through the Table 4 pathway and tells you the correct interval or referral.

Incidental findings

Every incidental finding from Table 1 of the QA Standards, searchable and filterable by body system. Each shows reliability of detection, what to report, management, and the onward pathway from Annex 3.

Flashcards

Tap to flip. Covers every nodule pathway, every incidental finding, and the key numbers you need to know. Filter by topic, mark cards you've learned, and shuffle the deck. Works brilliantly on the phone for a five-minute refresher.

Quiz

Clinical scenarios with immediate feedback — 25 cases mixing nodule management and incidental-finding decisions. Useful for PERFECTS-style calibration practice.

Reference

Quick-lookup values: size thresholds, VDT cut-offs, Brock risk, scan interval windows.

Sources

Built from two NHS England documents you supplied:

  • Targeted Screening for Lung Cancer with Low Radiation Dose Computed Tomography: Standard Protocol (B1646, Feb 2025) — sections 3–6 and Table 4.
  • Quality Assurance Standards (B1647, Feb 2025) — Annex 1 (reporting proforma), Annex 2 (incidental findings protocol & Table 1), Annex 3 (national IF pathways).

This is a learning aid, not a clinical decision system. Always consult the source documents and your local Screening Review Meeting for actual reporting decisions.
